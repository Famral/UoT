Frequently Asked Questions

            

                

                    What is WHOIS?
                    
WHOIS is a query and response protocol that is widely used for querying databases that store the
                        registered users or assignees of an Internet resource, such as a domain name, an IP address
                        block, or an autonomous system. It provides information like domain ownership, registration
                        dates, and contact details.

                

            

            

                

                    Why is some WHOIS information private or hidden?
                    
Many domain registrars offer privacy protection services (often called "WHOIS privacy" or "domain
                        privacy"). These services replace the registrant's personal information with the details of a
                        forwarding service provided by the registrar, helping to protect against spam and identity
                        theft.

                

            

            

                

                    How accurate is WHOIS data?
                    
Registrants are generally required by ICANN (Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers)
                        to provide accurate contact information. However, the accuracy can vary. Privacy services can
                        also obscure direct contact details. For official or legal matters, relying solely on WHOIS data
                        might not be sufficient.
